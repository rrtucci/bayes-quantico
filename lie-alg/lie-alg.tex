\section{Generators (infinitesimal transformations)}

Consider Hermitian matrices $T^i\in \CC^{n\times n}$
for $i=1,2, \ldots, N$
that satisfy

\beq
\tr(T^i T^j)=K\delta(i, j)
\label{eq-gluon-mass}
\eeq

These matrices are called
the {\bf generators}
of infinitesimal transformations.

It's customary to choose 
generators so that  $K=\frac{1}{2}$.\footnote{For $SU(2)$,
it is customary to
use $T^i =\frac{1}{2}\s_i$,
where $\s_i$ for $i=1,2,3$ are the Pauli matrices.
For $SU(3)$,
it is customary to choose $T^i=
\frac{1}{2}\lam_i$
where $\lam_i$
for $i=1,2, \ldots, 8$ are the Gelll-Mann matrices.
For both of these choices,
$K=\frac{1}{2}$.}
However, we will often set $K=1$
for intermediate calculations
and restore $K\neq 1$ at the end by dimensional analysis. 
Just remember that each $T^j$ scales as $\sqrt{K}$. 
For example, given
the equation 
$\tr(T^iT^j)=\delta(i,j)$,
we know that when $K\neq 1$,
$\tr(T^iT^j)=K\delta(i,j)$
so both sides of the equation scale as $K$.

We will use
the following
scaled version of $T^j$
as a birdtrack. Define

\beq
(C_{Adj}^i)\indices{_b^a}=
\frac{1}{\sqrt{K}}
(T^i)\indices{_b^a}=
\frac{1}{\sqrt{K}}
\bcen
\xymatrix{
&a\ar[d]
\\
i\ar@{~}[r]
&T^i
\ar[d]
\\
&b
}
\ecen
\eeq
In the CC convention, we will always
start reading the indices 
of this node at the wavy undirected leg.

$Adj$ stands the 
Adjoint. In this node (vertex), an adjoint representation (adrep) particle (wavy line, gluon) is generated (released) by
a fundamental representation (funrep)
particle 
(straight solid line, arrow).

\section{Clebsch-Gordan matrices}

introduced in 
Ch.\ref{ch-clebsch-gordan}.

are a simple
kind of CG matrix,
one with 
\begin{itemize}
\item a gluon
(adrep) particle

a general $\lam$ rep
particle emanating 
from the $i$
index,
\item 
a particle
of the funrep
entering
and another leaving
the node,
instead of 
any number of
funrep particles entering and leaving.
\end{itemize}
